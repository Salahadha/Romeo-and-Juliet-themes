What is a theme


A theme in literature is a central idea or underlying message that a work explores about
human nature, society, or life. Unlike a simple subject ( "love" or "war"), a theme is a
complete thought or insight the text conveys, such as "Love often requires sacrifice" or
"Pride leads to destruction." Themes are not explicitly stated but are revealed through characters
' actions, conflicts, symbols, and key events. For example, in Romeo and Juliet, the recurring motifs
of light and darkness develop themes like "Passionate love can defy social hatred but at a tragic cost."
Themes resonate universally, inviting readers to reflect on deeper truths beyond the plot.
